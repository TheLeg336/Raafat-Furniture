# Product

## Register

brand

> Balanced, brand-primary. The marketing storefront (hero, About, Visit Us) sets the
> default voice; the shopping flows (Shop, Product, Cart, Account, Admin) inherit the
> same luxury feel but are held to product-grade usability standards so first-time
> visitors navigate without thinking.

## Users

Furniture shoppers — first-time visitors and returning customers — browsing on phone and
desktop, in English or Arabic (RTL). They arrive to discover pieces and judge quality and
taste before committing. Their journeys diverge:

- Some browse online, then **visit the physical showroom** to buy.
- Some request **shipping or pickup** for stock pieces.
- Some place **custom orders**, then take delivery or pick up.
- Payment may happen **on the site or in person**.

The interface must serve all four paths without forcing one. Many are not highly
technical; the path from landing to "I know what to do next" must be obvious.

## Product Purpose

Raafat Furniture is a bilingual luxury furniture storefront. It exists to make the brand
feel more refined, cleaner, and more elegant than every local competitor — turning a
first visit into desire and a clear next step (visit, order, or inquire). Success is a
shopper who trusts the brand on sight, finds the piece they want effortlessly, and knows
exactly how to get it.

## Brand Personality

Elegant, timeless-but-modern, premium. Quietly confident, not loud. Warm luxury over cold
exclusivity. Voice is assured and unfussy — it shows the furniture and gets out of the
way. Modern touches (subtle motion, tasteful glass, fluid transitions) are welcome where
they sharpen the experience, never as decoration for its own sake.

## Anti-references

- **Trendy AI-SaaS slop**: cream/beige bodies, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows over every
  section, identical icon-heading-text card grids, gradient text, decorative
  glassmorphism. Modern features (incl. *tasteful, purposeful* glass) are wanted — but
  they must read as craft, not template.
- **Generic IKEA / big-box**: cluttered product walls, loud sale banners, warehouse
  density, commodity feel.
- **Cold / sterile minimal**: empty all-white art-gallery space that feels lifeless. This
  is a warm home for furniture, not a museum.

## Design Principles

1. **The furniture is the hero.** Imagery and pieces lead; UI recedes. Chrome never
   competes with the product.
2. **Effortless for a first-timer.** Familiar, obvious navigation — a new visitor never
   has to learn the site. Clarity is the luxury.
3. **Quiet confidence over shine.** Restraint reads as premium. Gold and motion are
   accents, never the whole outfit; avoid gaudy opulence.
4. **Modern, but earned.** Glass, blur, and motion appear only where they sharpen
   perception or delight with purpose — never as reflexive decoration.
5. **One brand, two languages, full parity.** English and Arabic (RTL), light and dark,
   must each feel first-class — not a translation bolted on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** as the floor and meet applicable legal requirements: ≥4.5:1 body
text contrast (≥3:1 large text), full keyboard operability, visible focus, reduced-motion
alternatives for every animation, and complete RTL/LTR parity. Go modestly beyond where
it helps real shoppers (comfortable touch targets, color-blind-safe status colors,
careful Arabic type sizing) — without over-engineering. Every user gets a good experience
across both themes and both languages.
